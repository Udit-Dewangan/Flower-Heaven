Juliet Rose

A masterpiece rose that symbolizes beauty and rarity.

Beauty of the Juliet Rose

The Juliet Rose is renowned for its delicate, apricot-colored petals that gradually transition to a soft peach hue. This rose boasts a unique, lush bloom that is both full and romantic, making it a favorite for weddings and special occasions. Its intricate layers and sweet fragrance enhance its beauty, captivating anyone who encounters it.

Where It's Found

Originally bred in the UK by David Austin, the Juliet Rose can now be found in gardens around the world. It thrives best in temperate climates where it can receive plenty of sunlight. Home gardeners often cultivate this rose for its aesthetic appeal and the rich history behind its creation.

Why It's Rare

The Juliet Rose is considered rare due to the extensive time and effort it took to develop. Over 15 years were dedicated to breeding this rose, making it a symbol of dedication and artistry in horticulture. The meticulous process of cultivating and selecting the best specimens contributes to its scarcity, making it highly sought after by collectors and gardeners alike.

Blooming Season

The Juliet Rose typically blooms from late spring through early fall. During this period, the bush showcases its stunning flowers in abundance, providing a breathtaking display of color and fragrance. Each bloom lasts for several days, offering a long-lasting beauty in the garden.

Interesting Facts

  • The Juliet Rose made its debut at the Chelsea Flower Show in 2006 and immediately gained attention.
  • It is often referred to as the "$15 Million Rose" due to the investment made in its breeding.
  • This rose variety is favored by floral designers for its stunning appearance and fragrance.
