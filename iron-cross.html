Iron Cross (Oxalis deppei)

A captivating flower known for its unique foliage.

Beauty of the Iron Cross

The Iron Cross flower is renowned for its striking leaf pattern resembling a cross, with deep purple and green colors that create a stunning visual effect. Its delicate yellow flowers bloom in clusters, adding a cheerful contrast to the unique foliage. This combination makes it a popular choice for garden enthusiasts and indoor plant lovers.

Where It's Found

This rare flower is native to Mexico and is commonly found in areas with rich, well-drained soil. It thrives in warm climates, often seen in gardens and botanical collections. The Iron Cross is a popular choice for collectors due to its unusual appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Why It's Rare

The Iron Cross is considered rare due to its limited natural habitat and specific growing conditions. While it can be cultivated, it is not commonly found in wild settings, making it a sought-after specimen for collectors and enthusiasts. Overharvesting in some regions has also contributed to its rarity.

Blooming Season

The Iron Cross typically blooms in spring and summer, from March to August. During this time, its cheerful yellow flowers emerge, attracting attention and delighting flower lovers. The foliage remains vibrant throughout the year, making it an attractive plant even outside of blooming season.

Interesting Facts

  • The Iron Cross flower is often used in traditional medicine for its believed healing properties.
  • Its unique leaf shape allows it to store water efficiently, making it drought-resistant.
  • It is also known as the "Purple Shamrock" due to its distinctive leaves.
